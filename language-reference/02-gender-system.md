# Layer 2: Gender System (Like German, But Simpler)

**Goal:** Understand how gender works so you can predict *el/la* and match adjectives.

---

## The Good News

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** You already understand grammatical gender. Spanish has **2 genders** (not 3), and **no cases**. This is easier than German.

| German | Spanish |
|--------|---------|
| der Mann (m) | el hombre |
| die Frau (f) | la mujer |
| ~~das Kind (n)~~ | *(no neuter!)* |

---

## Articles: el / la / los / las (Definite — "the")

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|-----------|----------|
| **Singular** | **el** café | **la** casa |
| **Plural** | **los** cafés | **las** casas |

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Like *der/die*, but no *das*. And articles don't change by case (no *den/dem/des*)!

---

## Indefinite Articles: un / una (— "a" / "an")

When saying "a coffee" or "a house," you use **un** or **una** — matched to the noun's gender:

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|-----------|----------|
| **Singular** | **un** café | **una** casa |
| **Plural** | **unos** cafés | **unas** casas |

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Like *ein / eine*:
- *ein Kaffee* → **un café**
- *eine Tasse* → **una taza**
- *ein Bier* → **una cerveza** (feminine! must check)

### ⚠️ The "une" Trap (Session 2 Error)

**`une` does not exist in Spanish.** If you produce it, it's a German/French mix bleeding through:

| What came out | Why it happened | Correct form |
|---|---|---|
| `une grande cafe` | *eine* (German) confused with French *une* | `un café grande` |

**Three things broke in that phrase:**
1. `une` → `un` — masculine noun, no feminine -e ending
2. `grande cafe` → `café grande` — adjective goes AFTER (see Sentence Structure)
3. `cafe` → `café` — accent mark changes pronunciation to kah-FEH

**Rule:** If you catch yourself adding an "e" to "un," stop. The masculine indefinite article is just `un`. Always.

### Quick Check: el/la vs un/una

- **el/la** = "the" (specific, definite): *el café que pedí* (the coffee I ordered)
- **un/una** = "a" (nonspecific, indefinite): *quiero **un** café* (I want **a** coffee)

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Same as *der/die* vs *ein/eine*. Exact parallel.

## Gender Rules (80% Predictable)

### Masculine (-o endings)
- Words ending in **-o**: *ninõ*, *gato*, *vino*, *tiempo*
- Exception: *la mano* (hand) — one of the few

### Feminine (-a endings)
- Words ending in **-a**: *casa*, *mesa*, *comida*, *persona*
- Exception: *el día* (day), *el mapa* (map), *el problema* (problem)

### Other Endings — Must Memorize
- **-ción, -sión, -tad, -dad** → Feminine: *la lección*, *la ciudad*
- **-ma** (Greek origin) → Masculine: *el problema*, *el sistema*, *el programa*

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Unlike German (*die Person* = f, *der Mensch* = m), Spanish uses **-a/-o** as main clue.

---

## Adjectives Must Match Gender & Number

### Examples

| Masculine | Feminine |
|-----------|----------|
| el café **caliente** | la casa **caliente** |
| el gato **negro** | la gata **negra** |
| los cafés **buenos** | las casas **buenas** |

**Rule:** Adjective ends in -o/a? Must match noun's gender + number.  
**Rule:** Adjective ends in -e or consonant? Same form for both genders.

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Like *der große Mann* / *die große Frau*, but simpler (no case changes).

## "Muchos" vs "Muchas" (From Today's Session!)

**Your error:** "muchos gracias"  
**Correct:** "muchas gracias"

Why? *Gracias* is **feminine plural** (from *la gracia*).  
So: *muchas* (feminine plural) must match.

| Singular | Plural |
|----------|--------|
| mucho café (m) | muchos cafés |
| mucha agua (f) | muchas gracias |

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Think *viel* (adj) → *viele* (plural). Same concept.

## Common Words You Must Know the Gender Of

### Masculine (el)
- el café — coffee
- el cambio — change (money)
- el día — day (exception!)
- el tiempo — time/weather
- el problema — problem (exception!)

### Feminine (la)
- la casa — house
- la mesa — table
- la leche — milk
- la tarde — afternoon
- la noche — night

---

## Articles with "a" Sound (Special Rule)

**Problem:** *la agua* sounds clunky (two "a" sounds together)  
**Solution:** Use *el* but word stays feminine

- ~~la agua~~ → **el agua** (but still feminine!)
- ~~la águila~~ → **el águila** (eagle)

**Proof it's still feminine:** *el agua fría* (not *frío*)

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Not like German *das Wasser*. Just a pronunciation trick.

## Practice: Fix the Gender Errors

1. ❌ el casa bonito → ✅ **la casa bonita**
2. ❌ la café caliente → ✅ **el café caliente**
3. ❌ muchos gracias → ✅ **muchas gracias**
4. ❌ un agua frío → ✅ **un agua fría** (agua is feminine!)
5. ❌ el problema grande → ✅ **el problema grande** (correct!)
6. ❌ une café → ✅ **un café** (une = not Spanish)
7. ❌ una café → ✅ **un café** (café is masculine)
8. ❌ grande café → ✅ **café grande** (adjective after noun!)
