# 13 — Mnemonic Dictionary

> The keyword method gives your brain a hook for every hard-to-remember word.
> Find an English word that SOUNDS like the Spanish word, then create a vivid mental image
> linking the sound-alike to the meaning. After a few retrievals, the keyword fades
> and the Spanish word connects directly to the meaning.

---

## How to Read This Dictionary

Each entry follows this format:

**Spanish word** (pronunciation) — *English meaning*
- **Keyword:** English word that sounds like the Spanish
- **Image:** A vivid, absurd, memorable mental picture linking keyword to meaning
- **Example:** A sentence using the word

The weirder, more vivid, and more personal the image, the better it sticks.

## How to Use This Dictionary

### During SRS Review
When a word keeps failing in your SRS cards, look it up here for a mnemonic hook:
1. Read the keyword and image.
2. Close your eyes and visualize the scene for 10 seconds.
3. Say the Spanish word 3 times while holding the image.
4. Return to your SRS card. The keyword will bridge you to the meaning.

### Creating Your Own Entries
The best mnemonics are YOUR OWN. This dictionary provides a starting library, but personal associations are stronger. When you encounter a hard word:
1. Say it out loud. What English word does it sound like?
2. Connect that English word to the Spanish meaning with an absurd image.
3. Write it down. The act of creation strengthens the memory.
4. Add it to this file or your personal SRS cards.

### Progressive Fading
After 5-10 successful retrievals using the keyword:
1. The keyword path will start to feel unnecessary.
2. You'll go directly from Spanish word → meaning.
3. This is the goal. The keyword is scaffolding — it falls away naturally.
