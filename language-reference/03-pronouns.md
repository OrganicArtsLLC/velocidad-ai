# Layer 3: Pronouns & "Tu" vs "Ti" vs "Tú"

**Goal:** Master the pronoun system, especially the confusing ones.

## Subject Pronouns (Who's Acting)

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Just like *ich/du/er/sie/wir/ihr/sie*

| Spanish | English | German | Example |
|---------|---------|--------|---------|
| **yo** | I | ich | **Yo** hablo |
| **tú** | you (informal) | du | **Tú** hablas |
| **usted** | you (formal) | Sie | **Usted** habla |
| **él** | he | er | **Él** habla |
| **ella** | she | sie | **Ella** habla |
| **nosotros** | we (m/mixed) | wir | **Nosotros** hablamos |
| **nosotras** | we (all f) | wir | **Nosotras** hablamos |
| **vosotros** | you all (Spain) | ihr | *(skip for now)* |
| **ustedes** | you all (Latin Am) | Ihr/Sie pl | **Ustedes** hablan |
| **ellos** | they (m/mixed) | sie (pl) | **Ellos** hablan |
| **ellas** | they (all f) | sie (pl) | **Ellas** hablan |

**Key difference from German:** Subject pronouns are usually **dropped** in Spanish!

---

## Subject Dropping (Different from German & English!)

In Spanish, the verb conjugation tells you who's acting, so you **omit the pronoun** most of the time.

### Examples

| With pronoun | Without (more natural) |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Yo hablo español | **Hablo español** |
| Tú hablas inglés | **Hablas inglés** |
| Nosotros queremos café | **Queremos café** |

**When to keep the pronoun:**
- For emphasis: **Yo** hablo español (pero tú no)
- For clarification: **Él** habla / **Ella** habla (both use same verb form)

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Unlike German (*ich spreche*, always with pronoun), Spanish drops it. More like Latin.

## The "Tu" Confusion (From Today's Session!)

**Your error:** "para tu"  
**Correct:** "para ti" or "para usted"

There are **3 different words** that sound similar:

### 1. **tú** (with accent) — Subject "you"
- *Tú hablas* — **You** speak
- Used as the **subject** of a sentence

### 2. **tu** (no accent) — Possessive "your"
- *Tu café* — **Your** coffee
- *Tu casa* — **Your** house
- Used to show possession

### 3. **ti** (no accent) — Object "you" (after prepositions)
- *Para **ti*** — For **you**
- *Sin **ti*** — Without **you**
- Used after words like *para, sin, con* (BUT: *contigo* = with you, special form)

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Like *du* (subject) vs *dich/dir* (object forms)

---

## Prepositional Pronouns (After para, sin, con, etc.)

When you use *para* (for), *sin* (without), *de* (of/from), you need the **object form**:

| Subject | After preposition | Example |
|---------|-------------------|---------|
| yo | mí | Para **mí** |
| tú | ti | Para **ti** |
| usted | usted | Para **usted** |
| él | él | Para **él** |
| ella | ella | Para **ella** |
| nosotros | nosotros | Para **nosotros** |
| ustedes | ustedes | Para **ustedes** |

**Exception:** *Con* (with) combines:
- *con + mí* = **conmigo** (with me)
- *con + ti* = **contigo** (with you)

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Like *mit mir*, *mit dir*, but fused into one word.

## Fixing Today's Errors

### Error 1: "muchos gracias"
- *Gracias* is feminine plural
- Must match: **Muchas** gracias

### Error 2: "para tu"
- *Tu* = your (possessive)
- After *para*, need object form: **Para ti** (informal) or **Para usted** (formal)

### Correct forms for "keep the change"
1. **Quédese el cambio** — Keep the change (formal command)
2. **Para usted** — For you (formal, what you meant)
3. **No necesito cambio** — I don't need change (alternative)

## Tú vs Usted (Like German Du vs Sie)

| Tú (informal) | Usted (formal) |
|---------------|----------------|
| Friends | Strangers |
| Family | Elders |
| Kids | Bosses |
| Peers | Service workers (initially) |

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Exact same concept as *du* vs *Sie*.

**For McDonald's crew:** Start with **usted**, then they may invite you to use **tú**.

**Verb forms are different:**
- Tú **hablas** — You speak (informal)
- Usted **habla** — You speak (formal, uses 3rd person form!)
