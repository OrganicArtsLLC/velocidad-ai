# Core Vocabulary — 1000 Essential Spanish Words

A production-first dictionary organized by frequency and domain. Every word here earns its place by appearing in real conversation. Organized in bands: learn the first 100 and you survive; learn 300 and you converse; learn 500 and you're comfortable; reach 1000 and you're functionally fluent for daily life.

**How to use this file:**
- Scan for words you recognize. Skip them.
- For unknown words: read the example, say it aloud, then close your eyes and produce the word.
- Mark words you struggle with in your personal `progress/chunks.md`.
- Revisit weekly. Your SRS deck should pull from this list.

**Format:** `spanish` — english · (part of speech) · *example*

## Band 1: Survival Floor (1-100)

These 100 words cover ~50% of all spoken Spanish. Master every one.

### Verbs (The Engine)

- **ser** — to be (permanent) · *Soy estudiante.* (I'm a student.)
- **estar** — to be (temporary/location) · *Estoy bien.* (I'm fine.) · *Está aquí.* (It's here.)
- **tener** — to have · *Tengo hambre.* (I'm hungry — lit. "I have hunger.")
- **hacer** — to do / to make · *¿Qué haces?* (What are you doing?)
- **ir** — to go · *Voy al trabajo.* (I'm going to work.)
- **querer** — to want · *Quiero café.* (I want coffee.)
- **poder** — to be able to / can · *¿Puede repetir?* (Can you repeat?)
- **decir** — to say / to tell · *¿Cómo se dice...?* (How do you say...?)
- **saber** — to know (facts) · *No sé.* (I don't know.)
- **conocer** — to know (people/places) · *No conozco la ciudad.* (I don't know the city.)
- **dar** — to give · *Dame uno.* (Give me one.)
- **venir** — to come · *Ven aquí.* (Come here.)
- **poner** — to put · *Ponlo aquí.* (Put it here.)
- **ver** — to see · *Ya veo.* (I see / now I get it.)
- **hablar** — to speak · *¿Habla inglés?* (Do you speak English?)
- **comer** — to eat · *Vamos a comer.* (Let's eat.)
- **vivir** — to live · *Vivo aquí.* (I live here.)
- **necesitar** — to need · *Necesito ayuda.* (I need help.)
- **gustar** — to like (lit. "to please") · *Me gusta.* (I like it.)
- **llamar** — to call · *Me llamo...* (My name is... — lit. "I call myself...")
- **pagar** — to pay · *¿Dónde pago?* (Where do I pay?)
- **esperar** — to wait / to hope · *Espera un momento.* (Wait a moment.)
- **trabajar** — to work · *Trabajo aquí.* (I work here.)
- **pensar** — to think · *Pienso que sí.* (I think so.)
- **creer** — to believe · *Creo que sí.* (I think/believe so.)

### Nouns (The World)

- **hombre** — man · **mujer** — woman · **niño/niña** — boy/girl
- **persona** — person · **gente** — people (always singular: *la gente es...*)
- **cosa** — thing · *¿Qué es esa cosa?* (What is that thing?)
- **casa** — house · **trabajo** — work/job · **tiempo** — time / weather
- **día** — day (masculine despite -a: *el día*) · **noche** — night
- **vez** — time (occurrence) · *una vez* (once), *a veces* (sometimes), *otra vez* (again)
- **año** — year · **mes** — month · **semana** — week
- **agua** — water (feminine but: *el agua* for sound) · **comida** — food
- **dinero** — money · **lugar** — place · **parte** — part
- **nombre** — name · **amigo/a** — friend
- **familia** — family · **hijo/a** — son/daughter · **padre** — father · **madre** — mother
- **hermano/a** — brother/sister · **esposo/a** — husband/wife
- **problema** — problem (masculine despite -a: *el problema*)
- **mano** — hand (feminine despite -o: *la mano*)

### Adjectives (Description)

- **bueno/a** — good · **malo/a** — bad
- **grande** — big · **pequeño/a** — small
- **nuevo/a** — new · **viejo/a** — old
- **mucho/a** — much / a lot · **poco/a** — little / few
- **todo/a** — all / every · **otro/a** — other / another
- **mismo/a** — same · **mejor** — better / best · **peor** — worse / worst
- **primero/a** — first · **último/a** — last
- **bonito/a** — pretty · **feo/a** — ugly
- **fácil** — easy · **difícil** — difficult
- **rápido/a** — fast · **lento/a** — slow

### Function Words (The Glue)

- **y** — and · **o** — or · **pero** — but
- **que** — that / which / than · **de** — of / from · **en** — in / on / at
- **a** — to / at · **con** — with · **sin** — without
- **por** — for / by / through · **para** — for / in order to
- **no** — no / not · **sí** — yes · **también** — also
- **ya** — already / now · **más** — more · **menos** — less
- **muy** — very · **bien** — well · **mal** — badly
- **aquí** — here · **ahí** — there · **allí** — over there
- **hoy** — today · **mañana** — tomorrow · **ayer** — yesterday
- **ahora** — now · **después** — after / later · **antes** — before
- **siempre** — always · **nunca** — never · **todavía** — still / yet
- **este/a** — this · **ese/a** — that · **aquel/la** — that (far away)
- **algo** — something · **nada** — nothing · **alguien** — someone · **nadie** — nobody
- **cada** — each / every · **entre** — between / among
- **como** — like / as / how · **donde** — where · **cuando** — when
- **porque** — because · **aunque** — although
- **sobre** — about / on top of · **hasta** — until / even

### Question Words

- **¿Qué?** — What? · **¿Quién?** — Who? · **¿Cuándo?** — When?
- **¿Dónde?** — Where? · **¿Por qué?** — Why? · **¿Cómo?** — How?
- **¿Cuánto/a?** — How much? · **¿Cuántos/as?** — How many?
- **¿Cuál?** — Which? / What? (with ser)

## Gender Traps — Words That Break the Rules

| Word | Gender | Why It Fools You |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| el día | masculine | Ends in -a, but from Latin *dies* |
| el problema | masculine | All -ema words are masculine (el tema, el sistema) |
| el mapa | masculine | Greek origin |
| el programa | masculine | Greek -ema pattern |
| la mano | feminine | Ends in -o, but from Latin *manus* |
| el agua | feminine (!) | Uses *el* for sound (*el agua fría*) but adjectives are feminine |
| la radio | feminine | Ends in -o, but feminine in most of Latin America |
| el/la mar | both | Masculine in daily use, feminine in poetry/navigation |
