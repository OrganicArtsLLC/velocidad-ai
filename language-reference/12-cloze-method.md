# 12 — Cloze Method and Exercise Bank

> Cloze deletion is the single most effective flashcard format for language learning.
> Instead of "word → translation," you see a real sentence with one piece missing
> and your brain must *produce* the answer from context.
> This builds grammar, vocabulary, and intuition simultaneously.

---

## What Is Cloze Deletion?

A **cloze** (from "closure") is a sentence with a gap:

> Yo _____ (querer) ir a la tienda.

Your job: fill the blank. The surrounding sentence gives you context clues — you're not guessing blindly. You're using grammar, meaning, and pattern recognition to produce the answer.

**Why it works:**
1. **Active recall** — You produce, not recognize.
2. **Context embedding** — Grammar is learned inside real sentences.
3. **Pattern detection** — Your brain starts seeing the patterns after enough reps.
4. **Desirable difficulty** — It's hard enough to strengthen memory, easy enough not to frustrate.

---

## How to Create Cloze Cards

### From Friction (Best Source)

After a session where you failed to produce *"¿Me puede dar la cuenta?"*:

> ¿Me _____ dar la cuenta? → puede
> ¿Me puede _____ la cuenta? → dar
> ¿Me puede dar la _____? → cuenta

Three cards from one failure. Each tests a different skill:
- Card 1: modal verb (can/could)
- Card 2: infinitive after modal
- Card 3: vocabulary (the check/bill)

### From Chunks

Turn any phrase from `chunks/bank.md` into cloze cards:

> Original: *No te preocupes.* (Don't worry.)
> Card: No te _____.  → preocupes (reflexive verb form)

### From Patterns

Turn templates from `patterns/reference.md` into fill-in exercises:

> Pattern: Quiero + infinitive
> Card: _____ ir al parque.  → Quiero (I want to go to the park.)

### Rules for Good Cloze Cards

1. **One blank per card.** Multiple blanks = too many unknowns.
2. **Only one correct answer.** If multiple words fit, add more context.
3. **Test production, not recognition.** The blank should require *producing* Spanish.
4. **Use real sentences.** Textbook-perfect sentences feel fake. Use sentences from real interactions.
5. **Grade by tier.** Start with single-word deletions, progress to phrase deletions.

---

## The Five Cloze Types

| Type | What's Deleted | Tests | Difficulty |
|------|---------------|-------|-----------|
| **Vocabulary** | A content word (noun, verb, adjective) | Word recall in context | ★★ |
| **Verb Form** | A conjugated verb | Conjugation + subject agreement | ★★★ |
| **Function Word** | Article, preposition, pronoun | Grammar intuition | ★★★ |
| **Phrase** | A multi-word expression | Chunk recall | ★★★★ |
| **Transformation** | Entire clause restructure | Deep grammar + production | ★★★★★ |

## How to Use These Exercises

### Daily Practice (5 minutes)

1. Pick 10 exercises from your current tier.
2. Cover the answer with your hand or a card.
3. Say the answer out loud (don't just think it).
4. Check. If correct, move on. If wrong, say the correct answer 3 times.
5. Return to missed items at the end.

### Progression

- **Start at Tier 1.** You should get 8/10 correct before moving to Tier 2.
- **Mix tiers.** Once you're at Tier 3, still include a few Tier 1-2 items as warm-up.
- **Create your own.** The best cloze cards come from YOUR friction — sentences YOU failed to produce.

### Converting to SRS Cards

Any cloze exercise can become an SRS card in `srs/box1.md`:

```markdown
**Front:** Yo _____ ir a la tienda. (want)
**Back:** quiero
**Source:** Session 2026-02-19, McDonald's L1
**Type:** Verb form cloze
```

Paste this instruction to the agent:
