### 30–99 (tens + y + unit — two words)

| Tens | Spanish |
|------|---------|
| 30 | treinta |
| 40 | cuarenta |
| 50 | cincuenta |
| 60 | sesenta |
| 70 | setenta |
| 80 | ochenta |
| 90 | noventa |

**Pattern:** treinta **y** uno (31), cuarenta **y** cinco (45), sesenta **y** tres (63)

**Key difference from English:** The connector `y` (and) is always used: *noventa y nueve* (99).

### 100–999

| # | Spanish | Notes |
|---|---------|-------|
| 100 | cien | Alone or before a larger number (cien mil) |
| 101 | ciento uno | `ciento` when followed by 1–99 |
| 200 | doscientos/as | Gender agrees! (doscientas personas) |
| 300 | trescientos/as | |
| 400 | cuatrocientos/as | |
| 500 | quinientos/as | Irregular (not cincocientos) |
| 600 | seiscientos/as | |
| 700 | setecientos/as | Irregular (not sietecientos) |
| 800 | ochocientos/as | |
| 900 | novecientos/as | Irregular (not nuevecientos) |

**Gender rule:** Hundreds 200–999 have masculine (-os) and feminine (-as) forms.
- *trescientos dólares* (300 dollars — masculine)
- *trescientas personas* (300 people — feminine)

**Example:** 547 = quinientos cuarenta y siete

### 1,000 and Beyond

| # | Spanish | Notes |
|---|---------|-------|
| 1,000 | mil | Never "un mil" |
| 2,000 | dos mil | |
| 10,000 | diez mil | |
| 100,000 | cien mil | |
| 1,000,000 | un millón | Uses `un` — is a noun |
| 2,000,000 | dos millones | Plural: millones |
| 1,000,000,000 | mil millones | NOT "un billón" (false friend!) |

**False friend alert:** *un billón* = one trillion (10¹²), NOT one billion.

**`millón` is a noun:** Requires `de` before the thing counted.
- *un millón **de** dólares* (a million dollars)
- *tres millones **de** personas* (three million people)

But `mil` is NOT a noun — no `de`:
- *mil dólares* (a thousand dollars)

### Number Punctuation (Critical Difference)

| Convention | English | Spanish |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| Thousands separator | 1,000 | 1.000 |
| Decimal point | 3.50 | 3,50 |

- *El precio es $1.500,00* = The price is $1,500.00

## Ordinal Numbers

| # | Masculine | Feminine | Abbreviation |
|---|-----------|----------|-------------|
| 1st | primero | primera | 1.°/ 1.ª |
| 2nd | segundo | segunda | 2.° |
| 3rd | tercero | tercera | 3.° |
| 4th | cuarto | cuarta | 4.° |
| 5th | quinto | quinta | 5.° |
| 6th | sexto | sexta | 6.° |
| 7th | séptimo | séptima | 7.° |
| 8th | octavo | octava | 8.° |
| 9th | noveno | novena | 9.° |
| 10th | décimo | décima | 10.° |

**Shortening rule:** `primero` and `tercero` drop the -o before masculine singular nouns:
- *el **primer** piso* (the first floor)
- *el **tercer** día* (the third day)

**After 10th:** Spanish typically uses cardinal numbers.
- *el piso quince* (the 15th floor) — not *el piso decimoquinto*
- *el siglo veintiuno* (the 21st century)

## Telling Time

### Asking the Time

- **¿Qué hora es?** — What time is it?
- **¿A qué hora...?** — At what time...?
- **¿Tienes hora?** — Do you have the time? (casual)

### Structure

**1:00:** *Es la una.* (singular — "it is the one")
**All others:** *Son las + number.* (plural — "they are the...")

| Time | Spanish | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| 1:00 | Es la una | Singular — `la una` |
| 2:00 | Son las dos | Plural |
| 3:15 | Son las tres y cuarto | "and a quarter" |
| 4:30 | Son las cuatro y media | "and a half" |
| 5:45 | Son las seis menos cuarto | "six minus a quarter" |
| 6:10 | Son las seis y diez | "and ten" |
| 7:50 | Son las ocho menos diez | "eight minus ten" |
| 12:00 | Es mediodía / Es medianoche | noon / midnight |

### Adding Minutes

**First half of hour (1–30 min):** Son las [hora] **y** [minutos]
- *Son las tres y veinticinco.* — It's 3:25.

**Second half of hour (31–59 min):** Son las [next hour] **menos** [minutos to go]
- *Son las cuatro menos veinte.* — It's 3:40 (lit: "4 minus 20")

**Alternative (increasingly common):** Just use the exact number.
- *Son las tres cuarenta.* — It's 3:40.

### Time of Day

| Phrase | Meaning | Range |
|--------|---------|-------|
| de la mañana | in the morning / AM | sunrise–noon |
| de la tarde | in the afternoon / PM | noon–sunset |
| de la noche | at night / PM | sunset–midnight |
| de la madrugada | in the early morning | midnight–sunrise |

- *Son las ocho de la mañana.* — It's 8:00 AM.
- *Son las nueve de la noche.* — It's 9:00 PM.

### Time Expressions

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| en punto | on the dot / sharp |
| más o menos | more or less / around |
| a eso de las... | around (time)... |
| hace una hora | an hour ago |
| dentro de una hora | in an hour |
| cada hora | every hour |
| hasta las tres | until 3 o'clock |
| desde las dos | since 2 o'clock |
| a tiempo | on time |
| tarde | late |
| temprano | early |

## Days of the Week

| Spanish | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| lunes | Monday | From Luna (moon) |
| martes | Tuesday | From Marte (Mars) |
| miércoles | Wednesday | From Mercurio (Mercury) |
| jueves | Thursday | From Júpiter (Jupiter) |
| viernes | Friday | From Venus |
| sábado | Saturday | From Sabbath |
| domingo | Sunday | From Dominus (Lord) |

**Key rules:**
- Lowercase in Spanish (not capitalized)
- Masculine: *el lunes* (on Monday), *los lunes* (on Mondays / every Monday)
- No preposition needed: *Nos vemos el lunes.* (See you on Monday.)
- The week starts on Monday in Spanish-speaking countries

## Dates

### Format

**Spanish date order:** day / month / year (not month/day/year)

| Written | Read As | English |
|---------|---------|---------|
| 15/03/2026 | el quince de marzo de dos mil veintiséis | March 15, 2026 |
| 01/01/2026 | el primero de enero | January 1st |
| 25/12 | el veinticinco de diciembre | December 25th |

**Structure:** el + [cardinal number] + de + [month] + de + [year]

**Exception:** The 1st of a month uses the ordinal `primero`:
- *el primero de enero* (January 1st)
- *el dos de enero* (January 2nd) — cardinals for all other days

### Asking About Dates

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| ¿Qué fecha es hoy? | Hoy es el quince de marzo. |
| ¿Cuál es la fecha? | Es el quince de marzo de dos mil veintiséis. |
| ¿Cuándo es tu cumpleaños? | Mi cumpleaños es el ocho de febrero. |
| ¿En qué año naciste? | Nací en mil novecientos setenta y seis. |

### Saying Years

| Year | Spanish |
|------|---------|
| 1976 | mil novecientos setenta y seis |
| 2000 | dos mil (or el año dos mil) |
| 2025 | dos mil veinticinco |
| 2026 | dos mil veintiséis |

**No shortcut:** Unlike English "nineteen seventy-six," Spanish always says the full number.

### Useful Date Expressions

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| hoy | today |
| ayer | yesterday |
| anteayer | the day before yesterday |
| mañana | tomorrow |
| pasado mañana | the day after tomorrow |
| la semana pasada | last week |
| la semana que viene | next week |
| el mes pasado | last month |
| el próximo mes | next month |
| el año pasado | last year |
| el próximo año / el año que viene | next year |
| hace dos días | two days ago |
| dentro de tres días | in three days |

## Money and Prices

### Asking Prices

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| ¿Cuánto cuesta? | How much does it cost? (one item) |
| ¿Cuánto cuestan? | How much do they cost? (multiple) |
| ¿Cuánto es? | How much is it? (total) |
| ¿Cuánto vale? | How much is it worth? |
| ¿A cuánto está el kilo? | How much per kilo? |

### Saying Prices

**Structure:** number + currency

| Price | Spanish |
|-------|---------|
| $5.00 | cinco dólares |
| $3.50 | tres dólares con cincuenta (centavos) |
| $12.99 | doce noventa y nueve (casual) |
| €20 | veinte euros |
| $100 MXN | cien pesos |

**Casual shortcut:** Drop the currency and cents word.
- *Son doce cincuenta.* — It's $12.50.
- *Cuesta tres con veinticinco.* — It costs $3.25.

## Age

Spanish uses **tener** (to have), not **ser** (to be):

- **¿Cuántos años tienes?** — How old are you? (lit: "how many years do you have?")
- **Tengo cincuenta años.** — I'm 50. (lit: "I have 50 years.")
- **Tiene tres años.** — He/She is three years old.
- **Mi hijo tiene ocho años.** — My son is eight years old.

**Common mistake:** Never say *"Soy cincuenta"* — that means "I am fifty" (like the number itself).

### Age-Related Expressions

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| ¿Cuántos años tiene? | How old is he/she? (formal/3rd person) |
| Acabo de cumplir... | I just turned... |
| Voy a cumplir... | I'm about to turn... |
| Es mayor que yo. | He/She is older than me. |
| Es menor que yo. | He/She is younger than me. |
| De mi edad | My age / the same age |
| Cuando era joven... | When I was young... |
| De niño/niña... | As a child... |

## Temperature

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| ¿Qué temperatura hace? | What's the temperature? |
| Hace treinta grados. | It's 30 degrees. |
| Estamos a cero grados. | It's zero degrees. |
| Bajo cero | Below zero |

**Critical note:** Spanish-speaking countries use Celsius. 30°C = 86°F. When someone says "hace cuarenta grados," that's 104°F — very hot.

### Quick Celsius Reference

| °C | °F | Context |
|----|----|---------|
| 0 | 32 | Freezing |
| 15 | 59 | Cool |
| 20 | 68 | Comfortable |
| 30 | 86 | Hot |
| 37 | 98.6 | Body temperature |
| 40 | 104 | Very hot / Fever |

## Phone Numbers

Spanish phone numbers are typically read in pairs:

- **555-123-4567** → cinco cinco cinco, uno dos tres, cuatro cinco seis siete
- Or in pairs: cincuenta y cinco, cincuenta y cinco, doce, treinta y cuatro, cincuenta y seis siete

**Useful phrases:**
- *¿Cuál es tu número?* — What's your number?
- *Mi número es...* — My number is...
- *Te marco.* — I'll call you. (Mexico)
- *Te llamo.* — I'll call you. (universal)
