# Layer 5: Sentence Structure (Simpler Than German!)

**Goal:** Build correct Spanish sentences with natural word order.

---

## Basic Sentence Structure: SVO

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Spanish is **easier** than German here. No case changes, simpler word order, no verb-second rule.

### German vs Spanish Word Order

| Language | Structure | Example |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| **German** | Verb-second (V2) | Heute **spreche** ich Deutsch |
| **Spanish** | SVO (fixed) | Hoy **yo hablo** español |

**Key difference:** Spanish verb position is fixed (after subject), unlike German's flexible V2.

---

## Standard Word Order: Subject + Verb + Object

### Examples

1. **Yo hablo español** — I speak Spanish
2. **María vende café** — María sells coffee
3. **Nosotros queremos agua** — We want water

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Main clause = same as English. No inversion like German *Heute gehe ich*.

---

## Questions: Inversion or Intonation

### Two ways to ask questions:

**1. Intonation (most common)**
- Statement: *Tú quieres café* (You want coffee)
- Question: *¿Tú quieres café?* (You want coffee?) — SAME words, rising tone

**2. Inversion (formal/written)**
- *¿Quieres tú café?* (Do you want coffee?) — Verb before subject

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Like German questions (*Du willst Kaffee?* vs *Willst du Kaffee?*)

### Question Words

| Spanish | English | German | Example |
|---------|---------|--------|---------|
| **¿Qué?** | What? | Was? | ¿Qué quieres? |
| **¿Quién?** | Who? | Wer? | ¿Quién es? |
| **¿Dónde?** | Where? | Wo? | ¿Dónde está? |
| **¿Cuándo?** | When? | Wann? | ¿Cuándo vas? |
| **¿Por qué?** | Why? | Warum? | ¿Por qué hablas? |
| **¿Cómo?** | How? | Wie? | ¿Cómo estás? |
| **¿Cuánto?** | How much? | Wie viel? | ¿Cuánto cuesta? |

**Note:** All question words have accents. Always use ¿ and ?

---

## Negation: Just Add "No"

**Super simple:** Put *no* before the verb. That's it.

### Examples

| Positive | Negative |
|----------|----------|
| Hablo español | **No** hablo español |
| Quiero café | **No** quiero café |
| Necesito cambio | **No** necesito cambio |
| Tengo tiempo | **No** tengo tiempo |

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Easier than German *nicht* placement. Always directly before verb.

**Double negative (natural in Spanish!):**
- **No tengo nada** — I don't have anything (literally: "I no have nothing")
- **No veo a nadie** — I don't see anyone ("I no see nobody")

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Unlike German/English where double negative is wrong. In Spanish it's required!

---

## Subject Dropping (Critical!)

In Spanish, you **usually omit the subject** because the verb form tells you who's acting.

### With subject (less natural)

- Yo hablo español
- Tú quieres café
- Nosotros vamos a casa

### Without subject (more natural)

- **Hablo español**
- **Quieres café**
- **Vamos a casa**

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Unlike German which always keeps pronoun. More like Latin.

**When to include subject:**
- Emphasis: **Yo** hablo español (pero tú no)
- Clarification: **Él** habla vs **Ella** habla (same verb form)
- After *pero* (but), *porque* (because): Hablo español **porque yo** quiero

## Adjective Placement (After Noun)

**Rule:** Adjectives come **after** the noun (opposite of English/German)

### Examples

| Spanish | English | German |
|---------|---------|--------|
| café **caliente** | hot coffee | heißer Kaffee |
| casa **grande** | big house | großes Haus |
| libro **interesante** | interesting book | interessantes Buch |

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Opposite of German (adjective before noun).

### ⚠️ Most Common Word-Order Mistake (Session 2)

Both English AND German speakers put adjectives before nouns. Spanish puts them after. This will fight your instincts for weeks.

**From today's session:**

| ❌ What came out | ✅ Correct | Why |
|-----------------|-----------|-----|
| `grande cafe` | `café grande` | adjective AFTER noun |
| `grande café` | `café grande` | accent matters too: kah-FEH |

**Drill pattern:** Say the noun first, then describe it.
- café... ¿cómo es? ...grande → **café grande**
- casa... ¿cómo es? ...bonita → **casa bonita**
- pedido... ¿cómo es? ...especial → **pedido especial**

**Exceptions (adjectives before noun):**
- Numbers: **Dos** cafés
- Quantity: **Mucho** tiempo
- Demonstratives: **Este** café
- Possessives: **Mi** casa
- bueno/malo/grande: can go before OR after (*buen café* and *café bueno* are both used, but after is always safe)

## Present Progressive: Estar + Gerund (I am -ing)

For actions happening **right now** or **in an ongoing period**, use **estar** + **gerund** (the -ndo form).

### Forming the Gerund

| Verb family | Rule | Examples |
|-------------|------|---------|
| -AR verbs | Drop -ar, add **-ando** | hablar → habl**ando**, practicar → practic**ando** |
| -ER verbs | Drop -er, add **-iendo** | comer → com**iendo**, aprender → aprend**iendo** |
| -IR verbs | Drop -ir, add **-iendo** | vivir → viv**iendo**, escribir → escrib**iendo** |

### Estar + Gerund — Core Forms

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| **Estoy aprendiendo** | I am learning |
| **Estoy hablando** | I am speaking |
| **Estoy practicando** | I am practicing |
| **Estoy buscando** | I am looking for |

### The McDonald's Recovery Phrase (From Session 2)

**Production gap:** "I'm learning Spanish" — what do you say?

- ❌ `etudiante habla español` — means "student speaks Spanish" (wrong construction, missing 's')
- ✅ **`Estoy aprendiendo español`** — I am learning Spanish
- ✅ **`Estoy aprendiendo`** — I'm learning (sufficient without the noun)

When you break character: **"Perdón, estoy aprendiendo"** — Sorry, I'm learning.

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Like *ich lerne* (simple) vs *ich bin am Lernen* (progressive). German progressive is colloquial. Spanish present progressive is the normal way to say ongoing actions.

## Object Pronouns (Tricky!)

When you replace the object with a pronoun, it goes **before the verb**.

### Examples

| Full object | With pronoun |
|-------------|--------------|
| Quiero **el café** | **Lo** quiero (I want it) |
| Veo **a María** | **La** veo (I see her) |
| Compro **los libros** | **Los** compro (I buy them) |

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Like German *Ich sehe sie* (I see her).

**Object pronouns:**
- **lo** (it/him) — masculine singular
- **la** (it/her) — feminine singular
- **los** (them) — masculine plural  
- **las** (them) — feminine plural

---

## Prepositions + Infinitives

**Pattern:** Preposition + infinitive (not conjugated)

### Examples

- **Para** comer — In order to eat
- **Sin** hablar — Without speaking
- **Antes de** ir — Before going
- **Después de** trabajar — After working

🇩🇪 **German speaker:** Unlike German *zu + infinitive* (um zu essen). Spanish just uses bare infinitive.

---

## Common Sentence Patterns (From McDonald's Sessions)

### 1. Quiero + noun/infinitive
- Quiero **un café** — I want a coffee
- Quiero **pagar** — I want to pay

### 2. No necesito + noun
- No necesito **cambio** — I don't need change
- No necesito **ayuda** — I don't need help

### 3. ¿Puede + infinitive?
- ¿Puede **repetir**? — Can you repeat?
- ¿Puede **ayudarme**? — Can you help me?

### 4. Para + person
- Para **ti** — For you (informal)
- Para **usted** — For you (formal)
- Para **mí** — For me

### 5. Nada más (closing the order)
- **Nada más** — Nothing else (= "that's all")
- **Eso es todo** — That's everything
- ❌ `no algo más` — wrong; can't negate with *no* this way
- ✅ **Nada más** — this IS the negative form already

### 6. Estoy + gerund (explaining yourself)
- **Estoy aprendiendo** — I'm learning
- **Perdón, estoy aprendiendo español** — Sorry, I'm learning Spanish
